Human Rights in the New Era of Dog Love

By Anuradha Goyal | April 12, 2025

Urban Living and Shared Spaces

As cities grow denser, the balance between animal welfare and human rights becomes more complex. Stray dogs often occupy common areas, raising concerns about safety, hygiene, and access to public spaces for children, the elderly, and people with disabilities.

Ethics of Feeding and Relocation

Dog feeders and activists play a vital role in animal care, but their actions—such as relocating dogs or feeding them in residential zones—can unintentionally infringe on the rights of local residents. This raises questions about responsibility, regulation, and the role of the Animal Welfare Board of India.

Did You Know?

In 2024 alone, India reported over 22 lakh dog bite cases, including 5 lakh involving children.
